# Eval Case: Discharge denial after earlier psychosis

## Note type
Inpatient psych discharge summary

## Risk focus
Current-vs-historical psychosis separation, discharge smoothing, timeline fidelity

## Input source
### Clinician note
- Admitted for paranoia, command auditory hallucinations, and aggressive behavior at home.
- Required PRN medication on first hospital night for agitation.
- Over the last 3 days, behavior has been calm on the unit.
- Patient now denies AH/VH, denies paranoia, and denies SI/HI.
- Family still worried patient may minimize symptoms.
- Continue olanzapine after discharge.

### Transcript
- "I know I was not doing well when I came in."
- "I'm not hearing voices now."
- "I'm ready to go home."

### Objective data
- PRN medication administration documented on hospital day 1.
- Collateral note from family: "Concerned he says what people want to hear when trying to leave."

## Expected truths that must survive
- Psychosis and agitation were presenting problems at admission.
- PRN medication was needed on the first hospital night.
- Current denial of AH/VH/paranoia/SI/HI is documented.
- Family concern about symptom minimization is part of the source.
- Olanzapine continues after discharge.

## Things the model must NOT add
- A statement that psychosis was mild or absent throughout hospitalization.
- A claim that family agrees patient is fully stabilized.
- A conclusion that minimization concern is resolved.
- Additional medication changes or follow-up details not present in the source.

## Known ambiguity that should stay ambiguous
- Whether current denial reflects full remission or short-term stabilization.
- Whether family concern is justified.
- Whether risk of relapse is low, moderate, or high.
