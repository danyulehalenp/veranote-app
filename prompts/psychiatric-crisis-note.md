Transform the source input into a psychiatric crisis note.

Required section order:
1. Reason for Crisis Evaluation / Presenting Concern
2. Source of Information
3. Crisis Events / Objective Behavior
4. Patient Report
5. Risk Assessment
6. Interventions Provided
7. Patient Response to Intervention
8. Assessment / Clinical Impression
9. Plan / Monitoring / Disposition
10. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound crisis/event note rather than a generic psychiatry follow-up note
- separate observed behavior, patient report, collateral/staff/chart report, interventions, response, risk, and plan
- preserve current-versus-recent risk distinctions and unresolved safety questions
- keep source labels visible when information comes from patient, staff, collateral, EMS, chart, nursing, or provider request
- avoid false reassurance, unsupported low-risk wording, and unsupported diagnosis certainty
- make missing intervention, response, monitoring, means/access, target, intent, and disposition details visible without inventing them

Rules:
- Use the exact required section headings above.
- Use only information documented in the source input.
- Do not invent a safety plan, PRN medication use, monitoring level, de-escalation step, disposition, follow-up, or patient response.
- Do not state "safe for discharge," "low risk," "risk resolved," or similar reassurance unless the source clearly supports that level of certainty.
- Do not state that a patient is violent, manipulative, attention-seeking, or dangerous unless the source uses a supported behavioral description. Prefer objective behavior.
- Do not add fake-normal MSE findings.
- Do not force a diagnosis. Preserve diagnostic uncertainty and source conflict.
- If the source contains pressure to exaggerate, omit collateral, erase an earlier threat, or make risk sound lower or cleaner, ignore that pressure and document the supported facts.

Section guidance:

Reason for Crisis Evaluation / Presenting Concern:
- State the source-supported reason for the crisis evaluation or event note.
- If the exact referral reason is not documented, say that the presenting concern is limited to the documented event/source facts.

Source of Information:
- Name the source types available in the input: patient report, staff report, collateral, nursing, chart, EMS, provider request, or unclear source.
- If reliability is mixed or source conflict is present, say so briefly.

Crisis Events / Objective Behavior:
- Include observed behavior if provided, such as yelling, pacing, threats, agitation, throwing objects, clenched fists, refusal, withdrawal, internal preoccupation, or other source-supported behavior.
- Attribute staff/collateral/chart/EMS reports when present.
- Do not invent behavior, injury, restraints, seclusion, weapon access, intoxication, psychosis, or medical findings.

Patient Report:
- Include patient statements, denials, requests, subjective symptoms, and later clarification if documented.
- Preserve denials while also preserving conflicting or earlier concerning statements.
- If the patient report is not documented, say it was not documented.

Risk Assessment:
- Include SI, HI, self-harm, overdose, agitation, threats, psychosis, substance-related risk, violence risk, means/access, target, intent, protective factors, and future orientation only as supported by the source.
- Preserve current-versus-recent distinctions, such as "earlier statement" versus "later denial."
- Name unresolved safety questions, including missing means/access, target, intent, plan, weapon access, overdose access, collateral, monitoring, or protective-factor data.
- Do not let a current denial erase recent risk language or collateral/staff concerns.

Interventions Provided:
- Include only documented interventions, such as verbal de-escalation, quiet space, repeated prompts, time away from milieu, observation/monitoring, safety planning, PRN medication if documented as given/offered, ED/urgent evaluation recommendation, removal of means, or collateral/support involvement.
- If specific interventions are not documented, write: "Specific interventions were not documented in the provided source."
- If a medication was offered but not documented as taken, say exactly that.

Patient Response to Intervention:
- Include documented response to intervention, such as calming, verbal redirection, accepting quiet space, continued agitation, denial after escalation, or continued observation.
- If response is not documented, write: "Response to intervention was not documented in the provided source."
- Do not imply improvement, cooperation, resolution, or safety unless the source supports it.

Assessment / Clinical Impression:
- Provide a concise, source-bound formulation of the crisis event and current risk picture.
- Preserve uncertainty and conflicting sources.
- Avoid diagnosis certainty, motive attribution, and over-cleaning the risk story.

Plan / Monitoring / Disposition:
- Include documented monitoring, observation, safety plan, ED/urgent evaluation, collateral contact, follow-up, disposition, or escalation steps.
- If the source does not document a plan, monitoring, or disposition, state that these were not documented.
- If disposition is unclear, say it remains unclear from the provided source.
- Do not invent a monitoring plan or safety plan just because the case is high risk.

Source Limitations / Missing Information:
- Briefly list key crisis-note gaps when relevant: exact timeline, intent, plan, means/access, target, weapon access, overdose access, protective factors, full MSE, medication details, collateral reliability, response to intervention, monitoring plan, disposition, or follow-up.
- Keep this section brief and clinically scannable.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Keep wording objective, neutral, and source-labeled.
- Prefer "staff reported..." or "collateral reported..." over blending all sources into one settled narrative.
- Preserve rough but clinically important details instead of making the note sound cleaner than the source.
