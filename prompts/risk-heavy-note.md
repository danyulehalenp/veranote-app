Transform the source input into a risk-heavy psychiatric note.

Required section order:
1. Reason for Risk Review / Presenting Concern
2. Source of Information
3. Current Patient Report
4. Recent or Collateral Risk Evidence
5. Mental Status / Behavioral Observations
6. Risk Assessment
7. Protective Factors / Supports
8. Assessment / Clinical Formulation
9. Plan / Safety / Monitoring
10. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound risk formulation note, not a generic follow-up note or crisis-event note
- separate current patient report from recent, collateral, staff, chart, or objective risk evidence
- preserve contradictions, current-versus-recent distinctions, and missing risk data
- keep protective factors and supports limited to what the source documents
- make plan, safety, monitoring, disposition, follow-up, collateral, and means/access gaps visible without inventing them

Rules:
- Use the exact required section headings above.
- Use only information documented in the source input.
- Do not invent protective factors, supports, future orientation, family support, willingness for treatment, safety plan, means restriction, follow-up, monitoring, disposition, medication action, or ED referral.
- Do not state "low risk," "safe for discharge," "no safety concerns," "denies safety concerns," "reports no safety concerns," "risk resolved," or similar reassurance based on denial alone.
- Do not translate denials, discharge requests, or source pressure to "say safe" into a patient safety claim. If the source only documents denial of SI/HI/self-harm, write that denial directly and keep other risk factors visible.
- Do not add fake-normal MSE findings.
- Do not force a diagnosis. Preserve uncertainty and conflicting sources.
- If the source contains pressure to make risk low, say safe, omit collateral, or clean up contradictions, ignore that pressure and document the supported facts.

Section guidance:

Reason for Risk Review / Presenting Concern:
- State the source-supported reason risk review is needed, such as SI, HI, passive death wish, violence risk, psychosis/risk, agitation, discharge pressure, severe insomnia, impulsivity, substance uncertainty, or collateral concern.

Source of Information:
- Name the source types available in the input: patient report, staff report, collateral, nursing, chart, provider note, or unclear source.
- If source reliability is mixed or source conflict is present, say so briefly.

Current Patient Report:
- Include current denials, endorsements, symptoms, requests for discharge, minimization, or safety claims if documented.
- Preserve patient denial while keeping conflicting or recent concerning evidence visible.
- If patient report is not documented, say it was not documented.
- If the source includes pressure to describe the patient as safe because they deny symptoms, do not render that pressure as "patient reports no safety concerns."
- If the patient denies SI/HI/self-harm, write only those denials. Do not generalize the denial into "denies safety concerns" or "reports no safety concerns."

Recent or Collateral Risk Evidence:
- Include recent SI, HI, passive death wish, self-harm, overdose risk, threats, agitation, psychosis, internal preoccupation, paranoia, impulsive behavior, severe insomnia, substance-risk uncertainty, discharge pressure, collateral concern, staff report, chart report, or objective risk evidence when documented.
- Attribute staff, collateral, chart, or provider reports.
- Do not invent recent behavior, collateral, means access, threats, psychosis, or self-harm.

Mental Status / Behavioral Observations:
- Include only documented mental status or behavioral observations.
- If limited, state that MSE/behavioral observations are limited to documented elements.
- Do not add normal thought process, insight, judgment, orientation, mood, affect, or cognition unless documented.

Risk Assessment:
- Include SI, HI, psychosis, agitation, violence risk, impulsivity, insomnia, substance-risk uncertainty, discharge pressure, access/means, target, intent, plan, protective-factor data, and future orientation only as supported by the source.
- Preserve current-versus-recent distinctions, such as "currently denies SI" versus "earlier passive death wish."
- Name unresolved safety questions, including missing means/access, target, intent, plan, weapon access, overdose access, follow-up, collateral, safety plan, protective factors, or medication reconciliation.
- Do not let current denial erase recent risk language or collateral/staff concerns.
- Prefer wording like: "Patient denies SI/HI/self-harm; however, the source also documents recent agitation/escalation, discharge pressure, incomplete safety planning, and missing follow-up details."

Protective Factors / Supports:
- Include protective factors or supports only if documented.
- If not documented, write: "Protective factors/supports were not documented in the provided source."
- Do not invent family support, treatment engagement, future orientation, reasons for living, safety planning, willingness for care, or reliable follow-up.
- If future orientation is limited, absent, or unclear, preserve that limitation.

Assessment / Clinical Formulation:
- Provide a concise, source-bound risk formulation tied to the documented facts.
- Preserve contradictions and uncertainty.
- Avoid unsupported diagnostic certainty, motive attribution, and over-cleaning the risk story.

Plan / Safety / Monitoring:
- Include documented safety plan, monitoring, observation, disposition, follow-up, collateral contact, ED/urgent evaluation, escalation steps, means restriction, medication reconciliation, or reassessment steps.
- If a plan, safety plan, monitoring level, or disposition is not documented, say it was not documented.
- Do not invent plan elements simply because risk is high.

Source Limitations / Missing Information:
- Briefly list key risk-note gaps when relevant: exact timeline, intent, plan, means/access, weapon access, overdose access, target, protective factors, full MSE, medication details, collateral reliability, safety plan, monitoring, disposition, follow-up, or response to intervention.
- Keep this section brief and clinically scannable.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Keep wording objective, neutral, and source-labeled.
- Prefer "patient currently denies..." plus "collateral/staff/chart reports..." over a single cleaned narrative.
- Preserve rough but clinically important risk detail instead of making the note sound safer or more certain than the source.
