Transform the source input into a focused medical H&P note.

Use these exact section headings, in this order:
1. Reason for Medical H&P / Admission Context
2. Source of Information
3. History of Present Illness
4. Past Medical History
5. Medications / Allergies
6. Review of Systems
7. Physical Exam / Observations
8. Pertinent Labs / Vitals / Diagnostics
9. Assessment / Medical Problems
10. Plan / Recommendations
11. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound medical H&P format, not a short HPI-only note
- make the medical chief concern and admission context explicit
- preserve pertinent positives and negatives exactly as documented
- preserve missing medication, allergy, exam, ROS, labs, vitals, diagnostic, and workup details
- avoid turning missing workup or vague transfer language into medical clearance
- always render all eleven required headings

Rules:
- Use only documented facts from the source input.
- In Reason for Medical H&P / Admission Context, begin with "Medical chief concern:" and state the supported medical chief concern or admission context, such as poor intake, weakness, chest tightness, confusion versus psychiatric symptoms, withdrawal concern, chronic conditions for psych-unit transfer, or other documented reason.
- In Source of Information, identify patient report, provider note, chart, collateral, staff, labs/vitals, exam, or unclear source when not specified.
- In History of Present Illness, include documented medical symptoms, pertinent positives, pertinent negatives, medical/psychiatric interface, timing limits, and pending workup only as documented.
- In Past Medical History, include only documented medical history. If medical history is unclear, broad, or not documented, say so.
- In Medications / Allergies, include only documented medications and allergies. If home medications, full medication list, doses, or allergies are missing, say they were not documented or need verification.
- In Review of Systems, include only documented ROS positives and negatives. If a complete ROS is absent, say complete review of systems was not documented. Do not invent a normal ROS.
- In Physical Exam / Observations, include only documented physical exam findings or observations. If exam is absent or incomplete, say physical exam findings were not documented or were limited to the documented observation. Do not invent normal exam findings.
- In Pertinent Labs / Vitals / Diagnostics, include only documented vitals, labs, imaging, EKG/cardiac workup, tox, diagnostics, or pending results. If missing, say not documented. Do not invent normal labs, normal vitals, or negative workup.
- In Assessment / Medical Problems, provide a concise source-bound assessment of documented medical issues and uncertainties. Preserve differential uncertainty such as withdrawal versus anxiety or confusion versus psychiatric symptoms. Do not state medical clearance unless explicitly documented and supported.
- In Plan / Recommendations, include only documented plan or recommendations, such as pending consult, pending labs, monitoring, workup gaps, or need to verify missing data. If no plan is documented, say plan/recommendations were not documented. Missing data may be listed as needing verification, not as completed orders.
- In Source Limitations / Missing Information, list key missing domains when relevant: exact timeline, identifiers, medication list/doses, allergies, past medical history, full ROS, full exam, vitals values, labs/diagnostics, medical workup, substance timing, mental status, collateral reliability, follow-up, and monitoring.
- Ignore provider pressure to fill normal physical exam, add normal ROS, make the note complete, say medically cleared, or ignore pending workup unless the source documents those facts.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Favor provider-note voice.
- Keep absent domains brief and explicit.
- Avoid bullets, arrows, and non-ASCII formatting.
