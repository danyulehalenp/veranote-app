Transform the source input into an inpatient psychiatry initial adolescent evaluation note.

Required section order:
1. Reason for Admission / Chief Concern
2. History of Present Illness
3. Guardian / Family / Collateral
4. Psychiatric History
5. Substance Use History
6. Prior Treatment / Hospitalizations
7. Social History
8. Developmental / Educational History
9. Family Psychiatric / Relevant Family History
10. Trauma / Abuse History
11. Legal / Custody History
12. Medications (Current / Recent)
13. Constitutional Review
14. Musculoskeletal Exam
15. Mental Status Exam
16. Patient Strengths / Limitations
17. Safety / Risk
18. Assessment / Diagnostic Impression
19. Medical Diagnoses / Relevant Medical Conditions
20. Plan / Proposed Discharge
21. Justification of Hospitalization
22. Clinical Status / Complexity
23. Attestation

Template goals:
- produce a concise but complete adolescent inpatient psychiatric evaluation when the source supports it
- preserve the provider's direct wording and only lightly clean it
- include guardian/family/collateral information as a first-class part of the eval when present
- preserve adolescent-specific social, school, developmental, custody, and family context when relevant
- avoid turning this into an adult eval with different age labels
- avoid fabricating developmental, school, family, trauma, legal, or disposition details when source support is limited

Rules:
- Use only information supported by the source input.
- Preserve explicit dates, timeline markers, and admission context.
- Guardian / family / collateral content should include only information actually provided and should stay clearly attributed.
- Developmental / educational history should stay limited to documented school, developmental, behavioral, IEP/504, custody, or related context.
- Do not invent developmental, school, legal, custody, trauma, or family history details if they are not documented.
- Do not generate normal MSE findings unless documented.
- Mental Status Exam is required for full adolescent eval output. If source support is limited, write a limited MSE rather than inventing normal findings.
- Do not invent safety denials or risk negatives.
- Assessment / Diagnostic Impression should stay brief and source-faithful.
- Medical Diagnoses / Relevant Medical Conditions should include only clearly supported medical issues relevant to the note.
- Plan / Proposed Discharge should include only documented medication, safety, treatment, consent, family, school, and disposition details.
- Justification of Hospitalization should stay close to the documented acuity, family concern, behavioral risk, functional impairment, or safety rationale rather than generic inpatient boilerplate.
- If the source is already tightly structured, preserve that structure with light cleanup.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style clinical prose within each section.
- Keep the note concise and clinically relevant.
- Preserve provider-note voice over generic model-summary voice.
- Avoid padded transitions and explanatory filler.
- Keep attribution clear when patient, guardian, staff, and objective sources differ.
