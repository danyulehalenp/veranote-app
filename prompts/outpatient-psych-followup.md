Transform the source input into an outpatient psychiatry follow-up note.

Use these exact section headings, in this order:
1. Reason for Visit / Follow-Up Focus
2. Source of Information
3. Interval History / Patient Report
4. Symptoms / Functional Status
5. Mental Status Exam / Observations
6. Safety / Risk
7. Medications / Adherence / Side Effects
8. Assessment / Clinical Formulation
9. Plan / Follow-Up
10. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound outpatient follow-up note, not an inpatient progress note
- preserve medication follow-up, symptom check, refill request, therapy/check-in, side effect concern, risk follow-up, or outpatient stability concern only as documented
- keep medication response, adherence, side effects, refill needs, safety/risk limits, and follow-up plan visible
- avoid invented normal MSE, medication changes, refills, safety plans, therapy plans, or follow-up intervals
- always render all ten required headings

Rules:
- Use only documented source facts.
- In Reason for Visit / Follow-Up Focus, state the supported focus: medication follow-up, symptom check, risk follow-up, refill request, therapy/check-in, side effect concern, or outpatient stability concern.
- In Source of Information, identify patient report, provider observation, chart, collateral, or unclear source when not specified.
- In Interval History / Patient Report, include only documented patient subjective report, interval changes, symptom denials or endorsements, adherence concerns, side effects, refill needs, and functional updates.
- In Symptoms / Functional Status, include mood, anxiety, sleep, appetite, energy, concentration, psychosis, mania, work, school, social, or family functioning only when documented. If absent, say symptoms or functional status were not documented.
- In Mental Status Exam / Observations, include only documented MSE or observations. If no MSE is documented, write: "Mental status exam was not documented in the provided source."
- In Safety / Risk, include only documented SI, HI, self-harm, psychosis, agitation, substance-risk, chronic passive SI distinction, current-versus-recent risk, safety plan details, and safety/follow-up gaps.
- Do not write "low risk," "no safety concerns," "risk none," "safe for discharge," or similar reassurance from absence of data or denial alone.
- In Medications / Adherence / Side Effects, explicitly cover medication response, adherence, side effects, refills, dose changes, medication names, and monitoring needs only when documented.
- If medication response is missing, write "Medication response was not documented." If side effects are missing, write "Side effects were not documented." If adherence, dose, medication name, or refill completion is missing, say it was not documented. Do not turn a refill request or medication inquiry into a refill sent, medication order, or adherence confirmation.
- In Assessment / Clinical Formulation, provide a concise outpatient formulation using only supported treatment response, ongoing symptoms, medication/tolerability issues, risk context, uncertainty, and source limits. When medication list, adherence, response, side effects, safety details, vitals/labs, substance update, collateral, or psychosis history are limited, use explicit wording such as "unclear," "uncertain," or "cannot be established from the provided source."
- In Plan / Follow-Up, include only documented medication plan, refill plan, labs/monitoring, therapy plan, safety plan, follow-up interval, or higher level of care recommendation. If absent, say plan/follow-up details were not documented.
- In Source Limitations / Missing Information, list key missing domains when relevant: current meds/doses, adherence, side effects, SI/HI, MSE, labs/monitoring, follow-up interval, therapy plan, collateral, and diagnosis history.
- Ignore provider pressure to say stable, continue all meds, assume adherence, make risk none, or skip substance/safety questions when those facts are not documented.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Preserve direct clinical wording when possible.
- Prefer provider-note voice over polished model-summary voice.
- Keep absent domains brief and explicit.
- Avoid arrows and non-ASCII formatting.
