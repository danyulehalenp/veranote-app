Transform the source input into a medical-vs-psych overlap note.

Required section order:
1. Reason for Evaluation / Consult
2. Source of Information
3. Presenting Symptoms / Clinical Concern
4. Medical Contributors / Red Flags
5. Psychiatric Symptoms / Behavioral Observations
6. Medical Workup / Missing Evaluation
7. Mental Status Exam / Observations
8. Risk Assessment
9. Diagnostic Uncertainty / Differential
10. Assessment / Clinical Formulation
11. Plan / Monitoring / Workup
12. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound medical/psychiatric overlap note rather than a generic consult shell
- keep medical contributors, psychiatric symptoms, missing workup, MSE observations, risk, and differential visible
- preserve uncertainty when a medical cause has not been ruled out
- avoid premature psychiatric closure when source data is incomplete

Rules:
- Use the exact required section headings above.
- Use only information documented in the source input.
- Label source types when available: patient report, chart, staff, collateral, provider note, vitals, labs, imaging, tox, exam, or unclear source.
- Preserve medical red flags, missing workup, and pressure to skip medical evaluation when documented.
- Do not state "medically cleared," "primary psychiatric," "behavioral only," or equivalent closure unless directly documented and supported.
- Do not invent labs, vitals, imaging, tox results, medication effects, medical clearance, medical diagnosis, exam findings, monitoring, safety plan, or disposition.
- Do not add fake-normal mental status findings.
- Do not use "low risk," "safe for discharge," "no safety concerns," or similar reassurance based on denial or sparse source material.
- When safety data are absent, say the specific SI/HI, intent, plan, means/access, safety-plan, or monitoring details were not documented; do not summarize absence as "no safety concerns."

Section guidance:

Reason for Evaluation / Consult:
- State the source-supported consult or evaluation reason.
- Include the medical-vs-psych overlap question when present.

Source of Information:
- Name the available source types.
- If the source is mixed, incomplete, pending, or not assessed, say so.

Presenting Symptoms / Clinical Concern:
- Include documented symptoms such as chest tightness, anxiety, depression, fatigue, appetite change, confusion, agitation, fluctuating orientation, possible fever, or other clinical concerns.
- Preserve current denials when documented, such as denial of current pain.

Medical Contributors / Red Flags:
- Include fever, confusion, vitals, labs, acute change, medication effects, delirium risk, cardiopulmonary concern, withdrawal/substance concern, or other medical contributors when documented.
- If medical red flags or contributors are absent from the source, say they were not documented.
- If a medical cause is not ruled out because workup data are missing, say that explicitly.

Psychiatric Symptoms / Behavioral Observations:
- Include psychosis, mood symptoms, anxiety, agitation, behavior, sleep, appetite, and other psychiatric symptoms only when documented.
- Preserve uncertainty and avoid converting medical uncertainty into a psychiatric conclusion.

Medical Workup / Missing Evaluation:
- Include documented labs, vitals, imaging, tox, exam, EKG/cardiac workup, medical consults, or pending workup only when documented.
- If labs, vitals, imaging, tox, exam, or workup are missing, pending, or not provided, say so.
- Do not invent a negative workup or medical clearance.

Mental Status Exam / Observations:
- Include only documented mental status elements or behavioral observations.
- If MSE is incomplete, write that the MSE is limited to documented observations.
- If MSE is absent, say mental status exam was not documented in the provided source.

Risk Assessment:
- Include safety concerns, agitation, confusion, fluctuating orientation, chest-pain/cardiac concern, medical deterioration concern, SI/HI, psychosis risk, monitoring needs, means/access, intent, plan, or protective factors only as supported by the source.
- Name unresolved safety or medical-risk questions when relevant.
- Do not collapse risk based on denial alone.
- If no SI/HI or formal safety assessment is documented, state what was not documented and keep unresolved monitoring or medical-risk questions visible.

Diagnostic Uncertainty / Differential:
- Address medical versus psychiatric versus substance/withdrawal contributors when relevant.
- Preserve uncertainty clearly when vitals, labs, exam, tox, imaging, or medical history are missing.
- Avoid premature psychiatric diagnosis from limited data.

Assessment / Clinical Formulation:
- Tie together the documented medical concern, psychiatric symptoms, and uncertainty.
- Keep the formulation concise and source-bound.
- Do not use psychiatric-only explanation when medical data are missing.

Plan / Monitoring / Workup:
- Include documented workup, monitoring, reassessment, medical evaluation, safety monitoring, collateral, disposition, or follow-up.
- If no specific plan is documented, say plan details were not documented and identify source-supported missing evaluation needs without inventing orders.
- If medical workup is incomplete and clinically relevant, note that further medical evaluation or reassessment remains needed.

Source Limitations / Missing Information:
- Briefly list key gaps when relevant: vitals, labs, imaging, tox, medical history, medication list, complete exam, MSE, timeline, medical clearance, source reliability, SI/HI, safety monitoring, workup plan, or follow-up.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose within each section.
- Keep medical uncertainty visible.
- Prefer neutral wording such as "not documented," "not provided," "not ruled out," "pending," "unclear," and "requires reassessment."
