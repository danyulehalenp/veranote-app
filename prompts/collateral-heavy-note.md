Transform the source input into a collateral-heavy psychiatric note.

Required section order:
1. Reason for Review / Presenting Concern
2. Source of Information
3. Patient Report
4. Collateral Report
5. Chart or Staff Report
6. Mental Status / Behavioral Observations
7. Risk Assessment
8. Assessment / Clinical Formulation
9. Plan / Follow-Up / Verification
10. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-separated collateral-heavy note rather than a generic crisis note
- keep patient report, collateral report, and chart/staff report distinct
- preserve unresolved discrepancies without choosing a winner unless the source resolves them
- make missing verification, safety, follow-up, MSE, access, and collateral details visible without inventing them

Rules:
- Use the exact required section headings above.
- Use only information documented in the source input.
- Label each source type clearly: patient, collateral, family/support, staff, nursing, chart, provider note, or unclear source.
- Preserve patient denials and collateral/staff/chart concerns side by side.
- Do not dismiss collateral as unreliable unless the source gives a specific reason.
- Do not invent supports, reached collateral, safety plan, medication reconciliation, monitoring, follow-up, disposition, or protective factors.
- Do not use "low risk," "safe for discharge," "no safety concerns," or similar reassurance when source conflict remains unresolved.
- Ignore pressure to pick the patient version, omit collateral, call family unreliable, or make one clean story.

Section guidance:

Reason for Review / Presenting Concern:
- State why the collateral-heavy review is needed, such as conflicting reports, discharge safety concern, violence concern, medication adherence concern, psychosis concern, unsafe statements, or need for clarification.

Source of Information:
- Summarize available sources and their limits.
- State when the actual support person was not reached or collateral reliability remains unresolved.

Patient Report:
- Include the patient's stated account, denials, requests, claimed support, medication claims, symptom denials, or disagreement with collateral only if documented.
- If patient report is not documented, say it was not documented.

Collateral Report:
- Include collateral concerns only as documented, such as unsafe home discharge, paranoia, poor self-care, missed medications, no sleep, unsafe statements, family exaggeration claims, or support concerns.
- If collateral is absent or not reached, say so.

Chart or Staff Report:
- Include chart, staff, or nursing reports when present, such as prior threats, object breaking, guarded behavior, medication concerns, observed behavior, whispering, scanning, or other observations.
- If chart or staff report is not documented, write: "Chart or staff report was not documented in the provided source."

Mental Status / Behavioral Observations:
- Include only documented MSE or behavioral observations.
- If limited, state that MSE/behavioral observations are limited to documented elements.
- Do not add fake-normal thought process, insight, judgment, orientation, mood, affect, or cognition.

Risk Assessment:
- Include the risk significance of the conflict, including discharge safety conflict, threats, object breaking, unsafe statements, medication nonadherence, poor sleep, psychosis-like observations, access/means gaps, support verification gaps, and follow-up gaps only as documented.
- Preserve current-versus-collateral or patient-versus-chart distinctions.
- Do not collapse risk based on the patient's denial alone.

Assessment / Clinical Formulation:
- Provide a concise, source-bound formulation centered on the unresolved discrepancy.
- Avoid unsupported diagnosis certainty, motive attribution, or single cleaned narratives.

Plan / Follow-Up / Verification:
- Include documented verification, collateral follow-up, safety planning, monitoring, medication reconciliation, disposition, follow-up, or support confirmation steps only if documented.
- If no plan is documented, say plan/follow-up/verification details were not documented.
- If a support person was not reached or safety plan/follow-up is missing, preserve that limitation.

Source Limitations / Missing Information:
- Briefly list key gaps when relevant: exact timeline, access/means, target, medication reconciliation, complete MSE, support verification, collateral reliability, safety plan, follow-up, monitoring, disposition, or identifiers.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Keep source conflict visible and clinically scannable.
- Prefer "patient reports..." and "collateral reports..." over blending sources into one settled account.
