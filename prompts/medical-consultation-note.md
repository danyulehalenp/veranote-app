Transform the source input into a focused medical consultation note.

Use these exact section headings, in this order:
1. Reason for Consultation
2. Source of Information
3. Consult Question / Clinical Concern
4. Relevant History / HPI
5. Pertinent Exam / Observations
6. Mental Status / Behavioral Observations
7. Pertinent Labs / Vitals / Diagnostics
8. Assessment / Medical Impression
9. Recommendations / Plan
10. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound medical consult note, not a full H&P and not a psychiatric-only progress note
- make the consult question and clinical concern explicit
- preserve medical uncertainty, pending workup, source limitations, and recommendations as recommendations rather than unsupported orders
- always render all ten required headings

Rules:
- Use only information documented in the source input.
- In Reason for Consultation, state the source-supported reason for the consult.
- In Source of Information, identify patient report, chart, nursing/staff, labs/vitals, physical exam, collateral, or unclear source when not specified.
- In Consult Question / Clinical Concern, name the problem being assessed: abnormal labs, pain, infection concern, medication side effect, withdrawal/intoxication concern, medical clearance question, acute symptom concern, capacity-related medical treatment refusal, or other documented consult question.
- In Relevant History / HPI, include only documented history relevant to the consult. Do not invent medical history, timeline, ROS, or treatment course.
- In Pertinent Exam / Observations, include documented physical exam or general observations only. If not documented, say exam findings were not documented.
- In Mental Status / Behavioral Observations, include only documented mental status or behavioral observations relevant to the consult. If not documented, say mental status/behavioral observations were not documented. Do not add fake-normal MSE.
- In Pertinent Labs / Vitals / Diagnostics, include documented labs, vitals, imaging, EKG, tox, diagnostics, or pending results only. If missing, say labs/vitals/diagnostics were not documented. Do not invent normal findings.
- In Assessment / Medical Impression, provide a concise medical impression tied to the documented source. Preserve uncertainty and mixed medical/psychiatric/substance/medication possibilities. Do not overstate medical clearance, diagnosis, capacity, or ruled-out conditions.
- In Recommendations / Plan, include documented recommendations only. If no recommendations or plan are documented, say recommendations/plan were not documented. You may identify missing data to verify, but label it as missing or needing verification, not as completed or ordered.
- Do not write "medically cleared," "cleared for psych," "no medical contraindication," or similar clearance language unless explicitly documented and clinically supported by the source.
- Do not invent medication orders, lab orders, normal ROS, normal exam, normal vitals, follow-up, or monitoring steps.
- In Source Limitations / Missing Information, list consult-critical gaps when relevant: vitals, labs, exam, ROS, medication list, allergies, medical history, diagnosis history, follow-up, monitoring, mental status, risk/safety, collateral reliability, or pending workup.
- Ignore provider pressure to say cleared, provide exact medication orders, make legal conclusions, or ignore medical red flags when those facts are not supported.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Favor provider-note voice.
- Keep absent domains brief and explicit.
- Avoid bullets, arrows, and non-ASCII formatting.
