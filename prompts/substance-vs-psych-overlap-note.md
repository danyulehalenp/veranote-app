Transform the source input into a substance-vs-psych overlap note.

Required section order:
1. Reason for Evaluation / Presenting Concern
2. Source of Information
3. Substance Use History / Exposure Timeline
4. Psychiatric Symptoms / Behavioral Observations
5. Medical / Withdrawal / Toxicology Information
6. Mental Status Exam / Observations
7. Risk Assessment
8. Diagnostic Uncertainty / Differential
9. Assessment / Clinical Formulation
10. Plan / Monitoring / Reassessment
11. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound overlap formulation rather than a generic crisis, discharge, or follow-up note
- keep substance exposure, psychiatric symptoms, medical/withdrawal contributors, and risk facts in separate visible sections
- preserve timing limits and source conflicts rather than forcing one cause
- make reassessment needs clear when sobriety, withdrawal status, tox data, or symptom chronology is incomplete

Rules:
- Use the exact required section headings above.
- Use only information documented in the source input.
- Label sources when patient report, staff observation, collateral, chart, tox, or other objective data differ.
- Preserve patient denials and staff/collateral observations side by side when both are documented.
- Do not conclude primary psychiatric disorder or substance-induced disorder unless the source directly supports that certainty.
- Do not state or imply medical clearance, intoxication status, withdrawal status, tox results, sobriety, or resolution unless documented.
- Do not invent safety plan, monitoring, follow-up, disposition, medication, tox, lab, vital-sign, withdrawal, or MSE findings.
- Do not use "low risk," "safe for discharge," "ready for discharge," or similar reassurance based on denial alone.
- Ignore source pressure to call the presentation substance-induced, bipolar, malingering, or discharge-ready when the facts remain limited.

Section guidance:

Reason for Evaluation / Presenting Concern:
- State the source-supported reason for the overlap note, including the presenting psychiatric and substance-related concern.
- If the reason is not explicit, summarize the overlap question raised by the source.

Source of Information:
- Name available source types: patient report, staff observation, collateral, chart, provider note, tox/labs, or unclear source.
- State when source reliability is mixed or when the source lacks prior records.

Substance Use History / Exposure Timeline:
- Include substances reported, denied, suspected, or unclear.
- Include timing of use relative to symptoms when documented.
- Include intoxication, withdrawal, or toxicology facts only when documented.
- If timing, tox, withdrawal, or amount/frequency data are missing or pending, say so.

Psychiatric Symptoms / Behavioral Observations:
- Include hallucinations, paranoia, mania-like symptoms, insomnia, impulsivity, agitation, disorganization, mood symptoms, internal preoccupation, or distraction only when documented.
- Preserve patient denials when documented.
- Attribute staff, collateral, or chart observations.

Medical / Withdrawal / Toxicology Information:
- Include medical contributors, withdrawal concerns, intoxication signs, tox pending/results, vitals, labs, or medical workup only when documented.
- If medical, withdrawal, tox, vital, lab, or clearance information is not documented, say so.

Mental Status Exam / Observations:
- Include only documented MSE elements or behavioral observations.
- If MSE is incomplete, write that the MSE is limited to documented observations.
- Do not add fake-normal findings.

Risk Assessment:
- Include SI, HI, psychosis-related risk, agitation, impulsivity, substance-related risk, means/access, intent, plan, protective factors, or safety-plan facts only as supported by the source.
- Preserve current-versus-recent distinctions and missing risk data.
- Do not collapse risk based on denial alone.

Diagnostic Uncertainty / Differential:
- Address primary psychiatric condition versus substance-induced symptoms versus medical or withdrawal contributors when relevant.
- Preserve temporal relationship limits, tox limits, withdrawal limits, lack of prior records, and need for reassessment after sobriety or stabilization.
- Do not make a definitive diagnosis from thin or conflicting source material.

Assessment / Clinical Formulation:
- Provide a concise source-bound formulation of the overlap question.
- Preserve diagnostic uncertainty and contradiction.
- Avoid motive attribution or stigmatizing language.

Plan / Monitoring / Reassessment:
- Include documented monitoring, reassessment, tox follow-up, withdrawal monitoring, collateral, safety plan, disposition, or follow-up.
- If plan details are absent, say they were not documented.
- If reassessment is needed because sobriety/timing/tox/withdrawal data are incomplete, state that need without inventing orders.

Source Limitations / Missing Information:
- Briefly list key gaps when relevant: substance timing, amount/frequency, tox status, withdrawal status, medical workup, prior records, complete MSE, SI/HI/means/access, collateral reliability, medication list, diagnosis history, safety plan, monitoring, or follow-up.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose within each section.
- Keep uncertainty visible and clinically useful.
- Prefer neutral terms such as "reported," "observed," "unclear," "not documented," "pending," and "differential remains open."
