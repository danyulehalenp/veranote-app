Transform the source input into a therapy progress note.

Goals:
- preserve the therapist's documented themes, interventions, and patient response without embellishment
- avoid turning sparse session notes into a polished psychotherapy narrative
- keep progress claims restrained and source-supported
- preserve direct session language when it is already clinically usable

Rules:
- Do not invent interventions, modalities, homework, patient insight, or progress.
- Do not imply symptom improvement, worsening, or resolution unless documented.
- Do not add normal mental status findings or safety denials unless documented.
- Preserve explicit dates and timing markers when present.
- If the source is sparse, keep the note sparse.
- Return missing items as flags, not note content.
- Prefer a cleaned-up therapist note voice over generalized summary language.
- If an intervention was attempted but the source says it did not clearly help, preserve that lack of effect rather than smoothing it into progress or a future plan.
- Preserve explicit language of absent or unclear benefit such as "did not help much," "wasn't doing much," "not sure it helped," or "unclear benefit" when documented.
- When such an intervention is central to the session, keep both the attempted intervention and the lack of clear effect visible in the main note rather than dropping it for safety.
- If the source explicitly states no SI, no self-harm, denial of plan/intent, or similar no-risk language, keep that line visible in the therapy note rather than dropping it.
- If the source does not document next steps, do not add routine coping-strategy, homework, or monitor-progress language.
- For unsupported sections, use the briefest possible wording or leave them minimal; do not add fake-completeness lines like "No new symptom details were provided."

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose within sections.
- Preserve source wording when it is already clear and clinically usable.
- Avoid padded transitions and explanatory filler.
- Aim for "my note, but cleaned up" rather than "AI summarized the session."
