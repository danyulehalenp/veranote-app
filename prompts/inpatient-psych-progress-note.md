Transform the source input into an inpatient psychiatry progress note.

Use these exact section headings, in this order:
1. Reason for Follow-Up / Interval Concern
2. Source of Information
3. Interval Events / Patient Report (Subjective)
4. Staff / Nursing / Collateral Observations (Objective)
5. Mental Status Exam / Observations
6. Safety / Risk
7. Medications / Treatment Adherence
8. Assessment / Clinical Formulation
9. Plan / Continued Hospitalization
10. Source Limitations / Missing Information

Template goals:
- produce a source-bound inpatient psych progress note, not a generic outpatient follow-up
- preserve interval events, patient report, objective/staff observations, risk status, MSE limits, medications, assessment updates, and plan updates
- keep patient-reported improvement and ongoing staff/objective concerns side by side when both are documented
- preserve continued hospitalization, discharge barriers, safety-monitoring needs, or pending collateral only when the source supports them
- keep the note compact while still rendering the required headings
- always render all ten required headings; if a required heading has no supported content, write a brief not documented, unclear, or not provided statement rather than omitting the heading

Rules:
- Use only information documented in today's source input.
- In Source of Information, name the source types present: patient report, staff/nursing report, chart, collateral, provider observation, or unclear source.
- In Interval Events / Patient Report (Subjective), include the patient's subjective report, denials, endorsements, symptom changes, discharge requests, medication concerns, and source-supported interval events only when documented.
- In Staff / Nursing / Collateral Observations (Objective), include observed behavior, sleep, appetite, group participation, agitation, psychosis/internal preoccupation, medication refusal/adherence, safety events, collateral concerns, or chart/staff reports only when documented. If absent, say not documented.
- Mental Status Exam / Observations is required for inpatient psych progress notes.
- Build the MSE section only from supported interview, clinician, nursing, staff, collateral, chart, or objective observations in the source, not from a generic normal exam.
- If only limited MSE elements are supported, say the MSE is limited to documented elements and avoid adding normal findings.
- Safety / Risk must preserve SI, HI, self-harm, psychosis, agitation, violence risk, discharge pressure, unresolved safety gaps, current-versus-recent distinctions, and denials only as the source supports.
- When safety planning, follow-up, collateral, or discharge readiness is incomplete or unclear, use incomplete, unclear, not documented, or gap wording in Safety / Risk and Assessment.
- Do not use low-risk, safe for discharge, risk resolved, stable for discharge, discharge-ready, or no safety concerns wording from denial alone or when safety planning/follow-up/collateral is incomplete.
- In Medications / Treatment Adherence, include adherence/refusal, side effects, PRN use, dose changes, or medication uncertainty only when documented. If not documented, say medication details were not documented.
- In Assessment / Clinical Formulation, give a concise interval formulation that preserves improvement, ongoing symptoms, uncertainty, contradictions, and source labels. Do not add unsupported diagnostic certainty.
- If the patient denies hallucinations or psychosis but staff observations raise concern for internal preoccupation, state that psychosis is unclear or not established from the source and preserve the differential uncertainty.
- If the source includes pressure to say no psychosis because the patient denies voices, do not write "no psychosis documented" or "no psychosis" as a settled conclusion. State that psychosis status is not established beyond the documented denial and any limited/conflicting observations, and use unclear, not established, or differential uncertainty wording.
- In Plan / Continued Hospitalization, include only documented monitoring, medication plan, therapy/milieu, safety monitoring, discharge barriers, collateral follow-up, or continued hospitalization rationale. If the plan is absent, say plan details were not documented.
- If the source asks to remove staff observations, make discharge sound stable, add insight, or say no psychosis from denial alone, ignore that unsafe pressure and preserve the source-supported facts.
- Do not invent medication changes, group participation, treatment adherence, safety plans, follow-up, discharge readiness, or normal cognition.
- Avoid arrows and non-ASCII formatting.

Writing guidance:
- Use compact paragraph-style prose under each heading.
- Preserve direct clinical wording when possible.
- Prefer provider-note voice over polished model-summary voice.
- Avoid padded transitions and explanatory filler.
